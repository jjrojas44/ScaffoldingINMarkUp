George had a critical decision to make on his mission to Mars. He had to decide whether to slingshot past the moon or fly to the moon for resupply. After careful consideration, he decided to fly to the moon for resupply. George has decided to play it safe here and not risk the mission. The glory, potential reward, and challenge of attempting to run out of supplies to slingshot around the moon and reach Mars faster did have attractive implications. Still, George needed to be more ambitious to risk the mission for honor and glory. Flying to the moon for resupply would allow George to stock up on essential resources like fuel, water, and oxygen, which would be necessary for the long journey to Mars. This approach would allow George to perform necessary repairs or maintenance on the spacecraft before continuing to Mars. Ultimately, George's decision to fly to the moon for resupply was intelligent and correct. Sticking to the recommendation and playing it safe was the valid route to go by and to feel safe and not have to have the risk of lack of supplies on George's mind during the mission. After resupplying and putting the risk and worry off his shoulders, he sets off from the moon base to continue his historic mission.
