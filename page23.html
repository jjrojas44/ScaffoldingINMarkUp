George had been working tirelessly on the Mars mission. After discovering water and the possibility of life on the planet, he took a well-deserved break. He wanted to relax and enjoy some time off, away from work stress. As he sat on the beach, he couldn't help but think about the mysteries of Mars and the questions that remained unanswered. Where did all the water on Mars go? Scientists have been trying to answer this question for decades, hoping to understand how Mars became an arid wasteland. At the same time, its neighbor, Earth, kept hold of its water and became a biological paradise. Newly discovered rocks on Mars, with their histories wiped clean, are still considered good places to look for signs of ancient life. However, analysis of Martian sandstone suggests that the previously existing waters on the surface of Mars would have had too high a salinity to support most Earth-like life. Despite this, NASA has confirmed evidence that liquid water flows on Mars today, raising new questions about how long microbial life could have survived on Mars if it ever formed at all. As George enjoyed his vacation, he couldn't help but wonder what other secrets Mars held. He knew there was still much to learn about the Red Planet and was excited to see what discoveries would be made.
